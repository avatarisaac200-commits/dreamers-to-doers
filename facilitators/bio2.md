Olufolake Adegoke is a policy analyst and programme manager working at the intersection of governance, youth development, and social impact execution.

With experience spanning multiple NGOs, she has led and supported programmes from concept to delivery, developing a practical understanding of what it takes to structure initiatives, mobilise resources, and sustain impact beyond ideas. Her work has consistently involved navigating funding realities, building partnerships, and aligning stakeholders to move projects forward.

She served as Lead Organiser and Partnerships Lead for TEDxOAU, where she drove sponsorship and partner engagement, and has contributed to multiple fundraising and resource mobilisation efforts across initiatives, including leading campaigns that secured over $11,000 during global giving cycles.

Currently, she leads Policy Foundry Africa, where she is building and mobilising resources for a policy-focused platform from the ground up, translating vision into structure and structure into support.

Olufolake holds a degree in Biochemistry and is currently pursuing a Master of Public Administration (MPA), bringing a multidisciplinary lens to how ideas are designed, funded, and executed within the social impact space.
